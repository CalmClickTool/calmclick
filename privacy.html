Privacy Policy

CalmClick privacy policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

In one sentence

CalmClick checks links, messages, and error text only on your device. We do not upload, store, sell, or analyze your pasted content on a server.

What CalmClick is

CalmClick is a free website and optional browser extension that explains suspicious links, scam-like messages, and common computer errors in plain English.

Data we do not collect

  • We do not collect the links, messages, or error text you paste or right‑click to check.
  • We do not require an account, email, or login.
  • We do not use advertising trackers, analytics pixels, or fingerprinting scripts.
  • We do not sell personal information.

Website

When you use the hosted website, checks run in your browser with local JavaScript. Your pasted content is not sent to CalmClick servers for analysis (there is no analysis backend).

Like any website, the computer or service that hosts the static files may keep standard technical server logs (for example IP address and pages requested). Those logs are for operating the site, not for reading what you paste into the tools.

Optional preference: the “larger text” button may save a simple setting in your browser’s localStorage so the choice is remembered. That stays on your device.

Protection list updates (optional)

CalmClick can download an updated public pattern file (rules-latest.json) so new scam tricks can be recognized without you reinstalling the whole app. That request is a simple download of rules. It does not include the link, message, or error text you are checking.

Updated lists may be stored in your browser’s localStorage so they work on the next visit. You can still use CalmClick fully offline with the built-in list. Local/offline copies do not auto-phone home; use “Update protection lists” when online if you want the newest patterns.

Browser extension

The CalmClick extension can open a check screen for a link you right‑click or text you select. That content is passed only into the extension’s own pages on your computer so the same local rules can run. It is not transmitted to CalmClick for storage or remote processing.

Permissions used:

  • contextMenus — adds “Check with CalmClick” to the right‑click menu.
  • https://calmclicktool.github.io/* — only so the extension can download the public protection-list file (pattern updates). Not used to read your browsing or send pastes.

The extension does not request access to browse all websites, read your email, or modify page content.

Offline / local copy

You can download a local copy of CalmClick and open index.html from your computer. In that mode, nothing needs the internet after the files are saved (except links you choose to open yourself). This is the most private way to use CalmClick.

Children

CalmClick is a general-purpose safety helper. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes

If this policy changes, we will update the date at the top of this page.

Contact

CalmClick is a free community-minded project. For privacy questions about a deployment you host yourself, contact the person or organization that published that copy of the site.
